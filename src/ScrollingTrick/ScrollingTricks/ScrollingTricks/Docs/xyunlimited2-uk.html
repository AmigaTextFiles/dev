This algorithm and the one of Scroller_XYLimited are very similiar. Scroller_XYUnlimited has many things in common as well. So read these documentations first!

As in Scroller_XYUnlimited horizontal scrolling in the bitmap (!) is restricted. But this time videoposx ranges between 0 and BITMAPWIDTH * NUMPLANES - 1. So the covered area no longer just consists of one planeline but of one whole pixel line. Again videoposy is used for compensation. Since here we can do adjustments by one whole pixel line and not just one planeline, the unit of videoposy may remain one pixelline.

In this algorithm as well it may be necessary to do blockblits in two steps. But it is no longer necessary to exclude the extraline from blitting. The problem, that VIDEOSPLITTING cannot happen in the visible area of a rasterline can be solved in a much easier and faster way, too. It is no longer necessary to blit parts of the top bitmap into the extraline in each frame. Instead, when videoposx is corrected (and only then) depending on the scroll direction either the topmost pixelline of the bitmap is blitted into the extraline (when scrolling left) or the extraline is blitted into the topmost pixelline (when scrolling right).
